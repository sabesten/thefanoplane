The Fano Plane in Physics is a monograph exploring the thesis that the Fano plane — the smallest finite projective plane, with 7 points and 7 lines — encodes structural features of fundamental physics.

Starting from the algebraic structure of $\mathbb{Z}_7^3$ as a discrete pre-geometry, it traces a path through the exceptional Lie group $G_2$, the Yang–Mills–Geometry framework, and heuristic connections to the Standard Model's flavor structure.

The narrative begins with an ancient observation: the Luoshu, a 3×3 magic square from traditional Chinese mathematics, is the oldest known combinatorial structure over the finite field $\mathbb{F}_3$. Seven is the smallest prime that permits a Fano plane. From three to seven, from the Luoshu to the quarks — this is the arc of the book.

《Fano平面与物理》是一部探索如下论题的专著：Fano平面——最小的有限射影平面，7点7线——编码了基础物理的结构特征。

从 $\mathbb{Z}_7^3$ 作为离散预几何的代数结构出发，经由异常李群 $G_2$、杨-米尔斯-几何框架，直到标准模型味道结构的启发式联系。

叙事从一个古老的观察开始：洛书，中国传统数学中的3×3幻方，是有限域 $\mathbb{F}_3$ 上已知最古老的组合结构。7是允许Fano平面存在的最小素数。从3到7，从洛书到夸克——这就是全书的弧线。
