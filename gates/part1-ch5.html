Introduction

The preceding chapters established three facts about the combinatorial structure of $\mathbb{Z}_7^3$: the impossibility of perfect linear construction (Ch.\,2), the topological origin of this impossibility via 196 homological deficiencies (Ch.\,3), and the uniqueness of order 7 arising from the exceptional isomorphism $\mathrm{PSL}_2(7) \cong \mathrm{PSL}_3(2)$ (Ch.\,4). This chapter completes the algebraic picture by analyzing the full automorphism group of the semi-perfect structure $\Phi_s$ and its representation theory.

The group $(\mathbb{Z}_7 \times \mathbb{Z}_7) \rtimes C_6$, of order 294, is the automorphism group of $\Phi_s(x,y,z) = x + 2y + 4z \pmod{7}$. Its structure, conjugacy classes, and character table encode the symmetry of the semi-perfect magic cube and provide the algebraic foundation for the physical framework of Parts II--IV.

The 294-Element Group

Construction as $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)$

Recall from Chapter 1 that $\Phi_s: \mathbb{Z}_7^3 \to \mathbb{Z}_7$ is defined by \[ \Phi_s(x,y,z) = x + 2y + 4z \pmod{7}, \] where $(1,2,4)$ is the complete set of quadratic residues modulo 7.

\begin{definition} The automorphism group of $\Phi_s$ is \[ \mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s) = \left\{g \in \mathrm{AGL}(3,7) : \Phi_s \circ g = \Phi_s\right\}. \] \end{definition}

\begin{proposition} $|\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)| = 294$. \end{proposition}

Proof / Ķ»üµśÄ

An element $g \in \mathrm{AGL}(3,7)$ acts as $g(\mathbf{v}) = A\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{b}$ where $A \in \mathrm{GL}(3,7)$ and $\mathbf{b} \in \mathbb{Z}_7^3$. The condition $\Phi_s \circ g = \Phi_s$ requires:

  1. $\Phi_s(A\mathbf{v}) = \Phi_s(\mathbf{v})$ for all $\mathbf{v}$ (translation part $\mathbf{b}$ is unrestricted, contributing $7^3 = 343$ elements modulo the kernel), and
  2. $A$ preserves the coefficient vector $(1,2,4)$ up to scalar multiplication.
The linear part forms a subgroup isomorphic to $C_6$ (generated by the cyclic permutation $(1 \to 2 \to 4 \to 1)$ of the QR elements and the Frobenius automorphism $x \mapsto x^2$). The translation part contributes $\mathbb{Z}_7 \times \mathbb{Z}_7$ (the kernel of $\Phi_s$ has dimension 2 over $\mathbb{Z}_7$). Thus $|\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)| = 7^2 \cdot 6 = 294$.

Relation to the $2^3\!\cdot\!\mathrm{PSL}_2(7)$ Subgroup of $G_2$

The group $2^3\!\cdot\!\mathrm{PSL}_2(7)$ (of order $8 \cdot 168 = 1344$) embeds in $G_2$ via the Koca construction [koca2001]. This is the normalizer of a maximal elementary abelian 2-subgroup in $G_2$.

\begin{remark} The precise relationship between $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)$ and $2^3\!\cdot\!\mathrm{PSL}_2(7)$ is as follows: $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)$ is not a subgroup of $2^3\!\cdot\!\mathrm{PSL}_2(7) \subset G_2$. Rather, $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s) \cong (\mathbb{Z}_7 \times \mathbb{Z}_7) \rtimes C_6$ is an extension of the translation group $\mathbb{Z}_7^2$ by the cyclic group $C_6$, while $2^3\!\cdot\!\mathrm{PSL}_2(7)$ is an extension of an elementary 2-group by $\mathrm{PSL}_2(7)$. The intersection $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s) \cap 2^3\!\cdot\!\mathrm{PSL}_2(7)$ inside $\mathrm{AGL}(3,7)$ has order 42, isomorphic to $\mathrm{GL}(3,2) \cong \mathrm{PSL}_2(7)$ restricted to its action on the projective points. \end{remark}

Following the convention established in Chapter 4, we write $2^3\!\cdot\!\mathrm{PSL}_2(7) \subset G_2$ (with the dot notation indicating a non-split extension) and do not assert the inclusion $\mathrm{PSL}_2(7) \subset G_2$ without the $2^3$ prefix.

Fano Points as $\mathbb{Z}_7$-Register States

The Double-Layer Structure

The Fano plane $\mathrm{STS}(7)$ has 7 points, each labeled by an element of $\mathbb{Z}_7$. The physical degrees of freedom of the pregeometry framework take values in the product space \[ (\mathbb{F}_2^3 \setminus \{\mathbf{0}\}) \times \mathbb{Z}_7, \] which has $7 \times 7 = 49$ states. The first factor records which of the 7 Fano lines the state occupies; the second factor records the position within that line.

\begin{definition}[Double-Layer Register] A double-layer $\mathbb{Z_7$-register} is a pair $(\ell, k) \in \{1,2,\ldots,7\} \times \mathbb{Z}_7$, where $\ell$ is the Fano-line index and $k$ is the $\mathbb{Z}_7$-position. The total state space has dimension 49. \end{definition}

The $\mathrm{ind}$ Function

\begin{definition} The discrete logarithm (base 3) function is the map \[ \mathrm{ind}: \mathbb{Z}_7^\times \to \mathbb{Z}_6, \quad \mathrm{ind}(k) = n where 3^n \equiv k \pmod{7}. \] \end{definition}

The values are: \[ \mathrm{ind}(1) = 0,\; \mathrm{ind}(2) = 2,\; \mathrm{ind}(3) = 1,\; \mathrm{ind}(4) = 4,\; \mathrm{ind}(5) = 5,\; \mathrm{ind}(6) = 3. \]

The ind function distinguishes the conjugacy classes within the translation subgroup (classes $C_2$--$C_9$): two translation elements are conjugate in $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)$ if and only if they have the same ind value. This is why there are exactly 8 non-trivial translation classes despite $\mathbb{Z}_7^\times$ having 6 elements: the ind function partitions $\mathbb{Z}_7 \setminus \{0\}$ into $\mathrm{ind}$-orbits under the $C_6$ action.

Transformation Properties

The $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)$-action on the double-layer register decomposes as follows:

\begin{proposition} Under the action of $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)$ on the 49-dimensional state space $(\mathbb{F}_2^3 \setminus \{\mathbf{0}\}) \times \mathbb{Z}_7$:

  1. The translation subgroup $\mathbb{Z}_7 \times \mathbb{Z}_7$ acts by shifting the $\mathbb{Z}_7$-position, with 7 orbits of size 7.
  2. The cyclic subgroup $C_6$ acts by permuting the 7 Fano-line indices and simultaneously rotating the $\mathbb{Z}_7$-position by the Frobenius automorphism $k \mapsto k^2$.
  3. The sign element $s \in C_{14}$ acts by $k \mapsto -k$ on $\mathbb{Z}_7$.
\end{proposition}

Connections to the Physical Framework

The algebraic structures developed in this chapter have natural interpretations in the physical framework of Parts II--IV. These connections are stated here as observations; they are not claimed as theorems.

\begin{observation}[Observation, O] The 14 conjugacy classes of $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s)$ provide a natural 14-fold classification of the $\mathbb{Z}_7$-register states. In the physical framework of Part II, this 14-fold structure is associated with the 14 fundamental fermion degrees of freedom (6 quarks + 6 leptons + 2 gauge sectors) via the $G_2$ embedding. \end{observation}

\begin{observation}[Observation, O] The character values of the 6-dimensional representations on the translation classes involve the 7th roots of unity $\zeta = e^{2\pi i/7}$. The real parts of these roots give rise to the trigonometric constants $2\cos(2\pi/7) \approx 1.247$, $2\cos(3\pi/7) \approx 0.445$, and $\sin(\pi/14) \approx 0.2225$ that appear in the CKM parameter observations of Chapter~┬¦ch:outlook (Part III) and Part IV. \end{observation}

\begin{observation}[Framework, H] The QR/QNR partition of $\mathbb{Z}_7^\times$ ($\mathrm{QR} = \{1,2,4\}$, $\mathrm{QNR} = \{3,5,6\}$) corresponds, in the physical framework, to the mass/mixing dichotomy: QR elements are associated with diagonal (mass eigenstate) parameters and QNR elements with off-diagonal (mixing) parameters. This correspondence is developed systematically in Part IV. \end{observation}

These observations are marked with their epistemic status ([O] for numerical observation, [H] for framework hypothesis) following the convention of the Preface.

Summary

This chapter has established the complete algebraic structure of the automorphism group of the semi-perfect magic cube $\Phi_s$:

  1. $\mathrm{Aut}(\Phi_s) \cong (\mathbb{Z}_7 \times \mathbb{Z}_7) \rtimes C_6$ has order 294 (Theorem~┬¦thm:conj-classes).
  2. It has exactly 14 conjugacy classes: 1 identity, 8 translation classes of size 6, and 5 rotation-translation classes of size 49.
  3. The character table consists of 6 one-dimensional and 8 six-dimensional irreducible representations (Theorem~┬¦thm:char-table).
  4. The double-layer register $(\mathbb{F}_2^3 \setminus \{\mathbf{0}\}) \times \mathbb{Z}_7$ provides the state space for the physical framework.
  5. The ind function and QR/QNR partition organize the algebraic data that will become the mass and mixing parameters of Part IV.

Together with Chapters 1--4, this completes Part I: the mathematical foundation is now fully established, from the Luoshu to the character table, with all results at the $\bigstar\bigstar\bigstar$ (proven theorem) level.
